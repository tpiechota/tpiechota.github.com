eLoyals

College of Computer Training (CCT) final group project (the eLoyals). The name eLoyals was formed by adding the letter “e” from “electronic”, and the word “loyal” derived from the word “Loyalty”. The letter “s” was then added at the end, because “eLoyal” was already a registered domain name.

The project idea came from a possible problem identified by the group, which is carrying multiple loyalty cards creates a burden to individuals who are interested in collecting them. Basically, what this means is that most individuals have different loyalty cards for different stores, and this might be viewed as a problem as over the years, most of the stores have been providing loyalty cards to their customers, which has been filling up customer’s wallets. Some customers do not even bother to get a loyalty card just to prevent the inconvenience of taking it out every time the individual is doing a transaction at the counter.

To prove that the problem actually exists we conducted an extensive research to prove whether the problem really exists, including surveys and interviews with business owners, and also risk assessment and competitor analysis.

eLoyals System Overview

 

The eLoyals software was designed following HCI (Human-Computer Interaction) which was incorporated into every phase of the Systems Development Life Cycle (SDLC) where planning took place before and after every phase from start to finish.

 

For the development methodology we chose Agile - SCRUM methodology which uses iterative and incremental processes, known as sprints, rather than traditional sequential or 'relay race' approach to product development, which helped us (the TSAR team) respond to unpredictable evens and requirements changes and quickly adapt to those changes. It also focuses on delivering the highest business value in the shortest time and time was our biggest concern.

 

The application makes use of programming languages such as PHP, jQuery/JavaScript, Ajax and MySQL. eLoyals is built on the responsive Bootstrap 2 framework. The visual part of the application was created in Adobe Photoshop CS6 and Adobe Illustrator CS6 and GIMP

The eLoyals also utilizes QR-Code API to generate custom qr-codes required for the system to operate as intended.

Months of hard work, planning, stress and sleepless nights went into the entire project.

LinkToDrink

This website is a work of my friend and myself. The purpose of that project was to allow clubs, pubs, etc. owners in Ireland to register on the website and for a fee (periodical or a % from business done through the site) and allow people to pre-buy drinks offered by a club or a pub with a discount for the night out. Business owners could also advertise different events happening in a club or a pub. Unfortunately people - at least in Ireland - tend not to stay in one place for the whole night (not saying it is a bad thing ). Even though it could have been easily turned in to a solely advertisement board, the project was dropped. Good idea and intentions but lack of extensive market research caused the project to fail.

Some part of the main layout is of my work and the entire "Administrator" side including back-end engine based on PHP and MySQL. For all the graphics and icons I used Adobe Photoshop CS.
